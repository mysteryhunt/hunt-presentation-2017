Come to Hunt HQ to pick up baking soda, vinegar, and other supplies.

Rules

You must build a baking soda and vinegar volcano and demonstrate its eruption. Contact HQ to request a visit or for information on how to submit a video. Use the word “JUDGE” as your request.

N represents the number of registered players on your team. sqrt(N) should be rounded to the nearest integer.

To obtain a passing grade in the science fair, you must score N points total according to the scoring rubric below. Please show HQ what points you plan on claiming.

Each separate item can be claimed only once. For example, you cannot claim 20 points for running two instances of the ten-point item “a would-be victim escapes.”

Scoring

  1. For sqrt(N) points: A volcano whose eruption knocks over sqrt(N) distinct objects representing “victims.”
    1. For sqrt(N) bonus points: Each of the sqrt(N) victims is hand-decorated to represent a member of your team.
  2. For 5 points: A volcano whose lava breaches a dam or a levee. The structure should hold back the lava for at least 2 seconds before breaching; the failure must be set off by the lava or other automated internal mechanisms, not external intervention.
    1. For 5 bonus points: The dam holds back the entire lava outflow before failing.
  3. For 5 points: A volcano that erupts at least 2 times, with the subseqent eruption set of by a simple trigger accessed from the edge of the model. (A crank, a string, a button, etc.—not “pick up the baking soda box again”)
    1. For 5 bonus points: Each of the repeated eruptions breaches another dam or levee.
    2. For 10 bonus points: The repeated eruptions are from a single crater.
    3. For 15 bonus points: The repeated eruptions are triggered by a time-delay mechanism with no specific human intervention. (It is not “specific intervention” if the operator is performing some continuous action and the time-delayed 2nd eruption is one of the effects of this action.)
  4. For 3 points: the model includes examples of sedimentary, igneous, AND metamorphic rock.
  5. For 3 points: The presenter provides estimates of the Reynolds number, Lockhart-Martinelli parameter, and Manning roughness coefficient of the lava flow.
  6. For 15 points each:
    1. Without the presenter’s specific intervention, a would-be model victim who starts in the path of the lava makes an escape.
    2. The model includes a manometer capable of showing the presence of excess pressure in the vent, which the presenter should point out with a thematically-appropriate film quote.
    3. The lava follows a path that circles the vent 1.5 or more times.
  7. For 30 points: Without the presenter’s intervention, a group of immobile would-be victims is saved by a Rube Goldberg mechanism that redirects the lava flow; the final saving action must take place after the eruption has begun but before any of this victim-group has been knocked over.
    1. For 5 bonus points: the mechanism should include an object which occurs in the game “Mousetrap.”
  8. For 40 points: Characters from Journey to the Center of the Earth enter the depths of the model via one opening, then are ejected along with the lava via a different opening.
  9. For 70 points, do either of the following. There can be multiple operator interventions to trigger multiple actions but they must all be mechanisms accessed from the edge of the model.
    1. The model recapitulates the main plot of the film Dante’s Peak by representing, in order: two pre-eruption victims, the beginning of the eruption, the third victim (Ruth), the dam breach, the fourth victim (Paul), the protagonists escaping peril to shelter in a cave, the town’s destruction, the protagonists’ reemergence.
      1. For 20 bonus points, do the above without specific operator interventions as explained in rule in 3c.
    2. The model creatively recapitulates the plot of the all-star 1980 Irwin Allen flop When Time Ran Out, including four distinct events of your choosing. The presenters should recite dialogue/narration accompanying the eruption, which must include actual lines of dialogue from the film and which must pass the Bechdel test.
      1. For 20 bonus points, do the above without specific operator interventions as explained in rule in 3c.
