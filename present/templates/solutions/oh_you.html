Each of the phrases below the grid has been encrypted using a Caesar shift cipher; when decrypted, each is a phrase that clues a word beginning with OVER or UNDER. (The puzzle's title is a homophone for "O/U," an abbreviation for an "over/under" sports bet.) For each phrase, if the shift that decrypts the phrase is also applied to the grid above the list of phrases, the part of the clued word after OVER/UNDER can be found in the transformed grid word-search style. It is then natural to extract the letters that appear immediately OVER or UNDER that word (reading in the same direction as the hidden word). In the grids below, the hidden word is highlighted in red, and the extracted letters are highlighted in blue.

The lengths of the shifts are all different, forming a complete set of numbers from one to ten. Ordering the extracted letters from each shifted grid by the length of shift gives the string DIDNO/TBRI/NGSU/FFICI/ENTL/UGGAG/ESERV/EDONT/HERO/CKS. DID NOT BRING SUFFICIENT LUGGAGE clues the word UNDERPACKED, and SERVED ON THE ROCKS clues the phrase OVER ICE. The answer to this puzzle is PACKED ICE. (This is related to the word "block" in the flavortext, since packed ice is a type of block in the game Minecraft.)
