Each column contains two clues and they are paired across. One of each of the clues clues a word that is a name in something in math/science/politics that is two people’s names hyphenated like Hewlett-Packard. The other clue, which may come first or second, is a transdeletion of the other word. Reading down the transdeleted letters in order spells out NOT EVEN and CANDID OR OPEN. This gives another pair of clues for another hyphenated set. ODD and FRANK… missing a letter gives DODD-FRANK.
