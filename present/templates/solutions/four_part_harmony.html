Each quatrain in the verse (all of which have rhyme scheme ABBA) indicates a song by the group ABBA. Reading the first words in each line gives these instructions:

SWEDISH POP FOUR SOME
EACH FOUR LINES INDICATES
ONE OF THEIR HITS
LOOK AT LAST WORDS
FIND WHERE THEY'RE FROM
WHAT'S THE RHYME SCHEME
THAT GIVES THE GROUP

The last words in each quatrain are rhymes taken from a particular ABBA song. In order, these are:

"Waterloo": more/war, refuse/lose
"Angeleyes": way/say, river/shiver
"I Have a Dream": fail/tail, me/see
"Take a Chance on Me": try/lie, line/mind
"Eagle": spell/tell, wing/everything
"Ring Ring": right/night, call/all
"SOS": near/fear, understood/good

The first letters of these song titles spell WAITERS, the answer to this puzzle.
