This puzzle is about Johann Sebastian Bach—it's a bunch of chorale titles, but given in pidgin-translations of the sort a non-German speaker might generate from vague homophone guesswork. Actually, there are two things wrong with each translation: first, there are the awful translations; second, in each title one German word has been turned into a proper name by a single letter substitution.

The solver should figure out which titles have been butchered, then take the single letter that was used to make the proper name. Read in catalogue order (the standard Bach catalogue is the Bach-werke-verzeichnis, hence “Back work for Zeke? nixed”) we get the words SCHLUCK and SCHWALBE which are German words meaning SWALLOW which is the answer.
