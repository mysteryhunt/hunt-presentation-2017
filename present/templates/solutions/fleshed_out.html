In order to complete the grid, nine across entries must skip a square, and the intersecting down entries must have two letters entered into those squares.

These across entries all contain the names of types of meat, and the skipped squares fall within each meat. The meats are illustrated next to the grid; these meats are, in order from top to bottom, LIVER, EMU, BISON, LAMB, TRIPE, VEAL, PORK, TUNA, and HAM. (Some of these are admittedly tricky to differentiate just from the pictures. EMU, BISON, and VEAL are arguably the toughest to recognize, but in a worst-case scenario, the solver can just see which arrangement of those three gives a coherent clue phrase.)
