This puzzle takes the form of an Infocom-style interactive fiction text adventure game.

The game world is divided into four kingdoms. At the beginning of the game, the player only has access to the first kingdom (named “Crimsilon”), but as the player completes portions of the game, their “passport” is updated to allow them to explore more of the world and solve more challenges.

The kingdoms (and their challenges) in order are:

  1. Crimsilon: steal a tiara from a castle (thieving)
  2. Cerulia: rescue a king from the desert, by creating vehicles to reach him (crafting)
  3. Merrygold: conduct a series of trades to obtain a home (trading)
  4. Blanc: combine information gathered from the rest of the game to derive a secret message

Read through the game transcript at the bottom of this solution to learn how to complete each of these challenges, by gathering and using items and interacting with the non-player characters in the game.

Throughout the game, there are many discoveries that should suggest to the player that they are actually wandering around inside of a game of Settlers of Catan! These discoveries include:

  • the structure of the world (the player is always walking on roads on the edges of hexagons, and visiting locations at vertices of the hexagons, and the player may only travel “ahead to the left”, “ahead to the right” and “behind you”)
  • thematic elements (the presence of Catan terrain and Catan resources in the world)
  • the existence of four different “kingdoms” representing the four players of the board game, that each control a separate set of roads and settlements
