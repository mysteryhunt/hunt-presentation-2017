Each "radio" is a clip from a specific song by a specific artist (though some songs share the same title). The images at the bottom clue the artists that are used, obliquely in some cases, but assigning a particular letter to each artist. Using the images as a "key" allows the audio clip "cyphertext" to be decoded.

The key matches letters to artists/songs as follows:

  • A - Van Morrison, Brown Eyed Girl
  • B - REM, Radio Free Europe
  • C - Lisa Loeb, Stay (I Missed You)
  • D - Florida Georgia Line, Girl on the Radio
  • E - Queen, Radio Ga Ga
  • F - Regina Spektor, On the Radio
  • G - Autograph, Turn Up the Radio
  • H - Gym Class Heroes, Stereo Hearts
  • I - Jurassic 5, Radio
  • J - The Carpenters, Yesterday Once More
  • K - Sam Cooke, Having a Party
  • L - Elvis Costello, Radio Radio
  • M - Scissor Sisters, Tits on the Radio
  • N - Train, Hey Soul Sister
  • O - Wang Chung, Everybody Have Fun Tonight
  • P - Wall of Voodoo, Mexican Radio
  • Q - The Ramones, Do You Remember Rock N Roll Radio
  • R - Donna Summer, On the Radio
  • S - Talking Heads, Radio Head
  • T - The Buggles, Video Killed the Radio Star
  • U - Madonna, Turn Up the Radio
  • V - Jonathan Richman and the Modern Lovers - Roadrunner
  • W - Good Charlotte, Sex on the Radio
  • X - Twenty-One Pilots, Car Radio
  • Y - The Beach Boys, Fun Fun Fun
  • Z - Bowling for Soup, 1985

Using the key, the clips decode to the following: WOLFPACKLEADEREXAHERTZFREQUENCYBEAMPERUVIANCAPITALGREEKNYMPHCURSEDBYJUNO

That sequence of letters parses into clues leading to words used to code letters in the NATO Phonetic Alphabet (i.e., the actual "Radio Alphabet").

  • WOLF PACK LEADER = Alpha
  • EXAHERTZ FREQUENCY BEAM = X-Ray
  • PERUVIAN CAPITAL = Lima
  • GREEK NYMPH CURSED BY JUNO = Echo

The letters spell the answer, AXLE.
