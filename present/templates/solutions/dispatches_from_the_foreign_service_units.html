The puzzle is to identify a bunch of non-Latin words/character sets, and collect the Unicode characters used to write them. The table is a 5×16 grid which should lead you to identify three particular lists of Unicode characters: Latin from U+20, Cyrillic from U+410, Greek from U+3A0, Hebrew from 0x5C0, some Latin-1 supplement characters from 0x140, katakana from 0x30A0, and miscellaneous symbols from 0x2630.

The image clues should lead you to: the title MI-5 (including the hyphen!), the Russian word element spelled in capitals ЭЛЕМЕНТ, the number 28 in Hebrew as כח, the Japanese word chiketto in katakana, チケット, the capital Sigma Chi from the fraternity symbol, ΣΧ, two characters from the Phaistos disk 𐇗 and 𐇜, and finally the Polish town name (in all-caps) EŁK. Color in the blank grid wherever you find a character. The colored squares are pixels that spell the answer KILO.
