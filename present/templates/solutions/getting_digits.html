In this puzzle, there are seven sets of seven clues that need to be solved, with an odd looking phone number at the bottom. Somehow the clues have to yield digits, and the standard digital display used to represent numbers in simple computer displays is a 7-bit standard display. Each of the clues leads to an answer that contains either ON or OFF (ignoring spacing and punctuation) exactly once in it. Each of these can be converted into a single digit, each represented by the numbers 1 through 7 (from the clues).

Using the standard 7-bit display below, you can make the follow table to find the new numbers for the phone number.

  • 1 represents 6
  • 2 represents 0
  • 3 represents 3
  • 4 represents 2
  • 5 represents 9
  • 6 represents 5
  • 7 represents 1

(123) 445-6622 x1767

This dummy phone number is then converted to the actual number you want to dial: (603) 229-5500 x6151. When you call this number, you are given the answer to the puzzle, FLEXA.
